My name's Jake. I am a 22 year old mentor and advocate for young men across the country. I specialize in helping my boys focus on:

  • confidence & comfort with themself
  • navigating anxiety & mental pressure
  • balancing relationships
  • exploring college/career interests & defining priorities
  • public speaking & presentation skills
  • how to communicate when something feels wrong
  • outings & experiences when they just need someone to go with

In my teenage years I dealt with severe anxiety, depression, manic episodes, and hospitalizations. Thanks to nearly a decade of therapy, psychiatric medication, a caring support system, and most importantly, a deep self-awareness, I made a phenomenal recovery beyond my loved ones' expectations.

Since then, I've helped take boys from video game addictions to D1 scholarships, from detention to their dream school, and from fearing social settings to asking their crush to prom. I have also taken boys from shriveling at their diagnosis to conquering their disorder, and from battling severe crisis alone to being safe in the hands of the very medical professionals who took care of me when I was sick.

I am not a therapist or doctor. What I provide is a relatable perspective and actionable, structured advice based on my personal experience and what I learn from my clients. My real job here is to make sure that your boy does the important internal work before he reaches the point where I was, or before he's out in the real world with responsibilities that he's expected to handle without the proper foundation.

What's up man, I'm Jake.

If you're wondering why your mom or dad asked you to check out my site, I want to make something clear: I am not a babysitter, I am not a tutor, and I am not a therapist. I call this service "My Cousin Jake" because that's basically who I am to the guys I work with. Anyway, here's how I got here:

I'd spent most of high school either stressed or depressed. By the time I got accepted to Vanderbilt in senior year I just felt numb and frustrated. I couldn't appreciate what I had accomplished, I was dealing with some pain from an old relationship, and even though I had lots of friends and a great family, I felt like nobody really understood me. These problems snowballed after I graduated but I masked them by traveling and partying with my friends all summer until I eventually spiraled into a psychotic episode.

Very long story short, I got so sick that I believed someone was trying to kill me. I then faked my death, changed my identity, and went missing in a foreign country for days until my family found me in a mental hospital. Real life horror movie. I had no idea something like this could even happen to a kid like me. After that, I couldn't go to college, and I had to spend a bunch of time in treatment and recovery centers. Everyone just treated me like the crazy sick kid. It was miserable.

It took me a while to figure out who I actually am. What I actually want. How to talk about what goes on inside my head before it gets out of hand. How to act accordingly when things start to slip. Once I did that work, everything else slowly fell into place. I moved to New York, started making music, met a ton of awesome friends who inspire me, and started dating the girl of my dreams. Three years later, I've built a career that gives me purpose, and most importantly, I'm healthy and happy.

Internal stuff first. Everything else follows. That's what I help you with. We meet up or call, we talk, we might go do something. No bias, no agenda, no bigger plans that you didn't ask for. Just real conversations with someone who's been through it and came out the other side with a life he loves.

If you're open to this, fill out the Calendly and we'll hop on a call. No pressure. Just see if it feels right.

Thanks,

- Jake Fucci
